## 2024-05-15 - ARIA attributes and focus-visible on Custom Toggles
**Learning:** Adding custom expand/collapse toggles or standard buttons with `outline-none` severely breaks keyboard accessibility by removing focus indicators. The `outline-none` utility must be consistently paired with `focus-visible:` utilities (`focus-visible:ring-2`, `focus-visible:ring-offset-2`) to preserve screen reader usability and keyboard navigation without breaking mouse click visual design. Additionally, toggles lacking `aria-expanded` and `aria-controls` states give zero context to screen readers on state changes.
**Action:** Always scan for `outline-none` classes on interactive elements and pair them with `focus-visible:ring-2`. Add `aria-expanded` and `aria-controls` to any expanding toggle elements along with `id` bindings to their associated containers. Use `aria-busy` for buttons managing loading states.
